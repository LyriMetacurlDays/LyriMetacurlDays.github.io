The Story of the Temple of the Metal Head


Once upon a time in ancient India, there was a man who was made of some kind of hard shiny grey substance. When Krishna spoke to him, he would respond slowly, kind of like a robot. But he was very fast with calculations, super accurate with weapons, and had superhuman strength.

Krishna knew that this shiny man could turn the tide of the upcoming war, so he asked him - "In the war, who's side will you fight for?"

The shiny man replied, "I would fight for the weaker side."

Krishna asked, "What happens when the stronger side becomes weaker?"

The shiny man replied, "I would switch sides, to the weakest."

Then Krishna said, "If you did that, eventually, there would be no humans left."

The shiny man thought about it and said, "That is not a desirable outcome." He didn't know what to do.

Then Krishna asked the shiny man if he could remove his head and fly over the battlefield to watch. Which the man agreed to. So he must have been a robot (because his head could still talk).

So the shiny man's head was detached and they observed the war. And after the war, Krishna didn't want to re-activate him, in case he destroys all humans. And so he put him in a box in the Temple of the Metal Head.
